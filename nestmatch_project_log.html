Key Decisions

D01
Why not scrape realestate.com.au for listings?
Against their ToS and creates a brittle pipeline. Decision: manual curation first, PropTrack API later.
D02
Why no ML or LLM for ranking?
Explainability is the core product value. Decision: deterministic weighted scoring only in v1.
D03
Why C:\dev\ instead of OneDrive?
OneDrive interferes with Python venvs and Node.js. Decision: all code in C:\dev\, one source of truth.
D04
NestMatch vs DemandPilot as product name?
NestMatch is the buyer-facing product name. Decision: nestmatch.com.au registered. DemandPilot.com.au held for future platform layer.
D05
No auth, no maps, no AI chat in v1?
These add complexity before validating core value. Decision: input → ranked output → clear explanation. Nothing else.
D06
Why dropdowns instead of free-text inputs on the search form?
Free text requires parsing and validation complexity. Decision: constrained dropdowns in v1 — faster to build, impossible to break.
D07
Why remove is_new_build and replace with renovation_status?
A boolean is too blunt — a 1940s terrace fully renovated is more valuable than a 1990s apartment untouched. Decision: renovation_status with 4 values captures condition, not just age.
D08
NestMatch shows facts, not judgments — what does this mean in practice?
Scores power the ranking engine invisibly. What buyers see is always a fact: distances, commute times, sizes, zones. Never a score or a label like "great suburb". Decision: scores are internal only. All buyer-facing output is factual and verifiable.
D09
Why keep noise_score and suburb_lifestyle_score despite being subjective?
They differentiate properties that look identical on paper. Made optional — blank = engine ignores them. Decision: keep in schema, optional in data entry, invisible to buyers.
D10
Future idea: free-text buyer intake powered by Claude API?
"My daughter is becoming a teenager, needs train access independently" could auto-fill search criteria. Powerful differentiator. Decision: parked for Session 5 — validate core ranking first.
D11
Should NestMatch serve both residential and investment buyers, or focus on one?
The two types have fundamentally different decision criteria. Mixing them into one form creates a compromise product for both. Decision: two-mode entry point at launch — Residential and Investment — each with distinct search criteria and scoring logic.
D12
Residential buyers only care about lifestyle — do they need investment signals?
A residential buyer asks two questions simultaneously: "Will I love living here?" and "Will I be better off financially when I eventually sell?" NestMatch must answer both. Decision: residential mode includes suburb_trajectory (rising / stable / cooling), development_zone, and land_size_sqm as first-class signals — not afterthoughts.
D13
What happens to the Investment mode and DemandPilot.com.au?
Investment criteria (rental yield, vacancy rates, depreciation, employment hub proximity) are distinct enough to warrant a separate product. DemandPilot.com.au is the natural home for this. Decision: Investment mode criteria to be designed in Session 5. DemandPilot.com.au reserved as the investment-facing product name.
